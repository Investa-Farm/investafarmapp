1. Investor Performance & Portfolio Metrics

1.1 Return on Investment (ROI)

Formula:

\text{ROI} = \frac{\text{Current Value} - \text{Cost Basis}}{\text{Cost Basis}} \times 100

For a single farm:

· Cost Basis = shares × average purchase price (including fees)
· Current Value = shares × current market price (fair value or last trade)

For portfolio (multiple farms):

· Weighted average ROI across all holdings

What to tell Replit:

Calculate ROI for each farm holding and for the overall portfolio. Store historical ROI snapshots daily to show performance charts.

---

1.2 Annualized Return (IRR)

Since farm investments are seasonal (3–9 months), ROI alone doesn't tell the full story. Annualized return lets investors compare across different time horizons.

Formula:

\text{Annualized Return} = \left(1 + \text{ROI}\right)^{\frac{365}{\text{days held}}} - 1

Example: 20% ROI over 120 days = (1.20)^{365/120} - 1 = 1.20^{3.04} - 1 = 78.9\% annualized.

---

1.3 Sharpe Ratio (Risk‑Adjusted Return)

Measures return per unit of risk. Essential for comparing farm investments and portfolios.

Formula:

\text{Sharpe} = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}

· R_p = expected return of the farm/portfolio (from forecast)
· R_f = risk‑free rate (e.g., 10% or current T‑bill rate)
· \sigma_p = standard deviation of returns (from historical or simulated volatility)

Interpretation:

· Sharpe > 1 = good risk‑adjusted return
· Sharpe < 0.5 = poor risk‑adjusted return

---

1.4 Win Rate (for farmers/investors)

Percentage of completed seasons that were profitable.

Formula:

\text{Win Rate} = \frac{\text{Profitable Seasons}}{\text{Total Seasons}} \times 100

Used for:

· Ranking farmers (trust score)
· Investor decision‑making (only invest in farmers with >70% win rate)
· Underwriting (risk score adjustment)

---

1.5 Maximum Drawdown

The largest peak‑to‑trough decline in value. Important for risk‑averse investors.

Formula:

\text{Drawdown} = \frac{\text{Current Value} - \text{Peak Value}}{\text{Peak Value}}

Implementation:

· Track daily portfolio value.
· Record the highest value ever achieved.
· Current drawdown = current value vs that peak.

---

2. Farmer & Farm Performance Metrics

2.1 Cost Per Kg Produced

Measures farming efficiency.

Formula:

\text{Cost per kg} = \frac{\text{Total Input Costs}}{\text{Total Yield (kg)}}

Components:

· Seeds
· Fertilizer
· Labour
· Irrigation
· Pest control

What to tell Replit:

Farmers input their cost breakdown. Platform calculates cost per kg and compares to regional averages. Lower cost per kg = higher farmer skill rating.

---

2.2 Yield Gap Analysis

Compares actual yield to potential yield (based on crop type, region, and weather).

Formula:

\text{Yield Gap} = \frac{\text{Actual Yield}}{\text{Expected Yield}} \times 100

Interpretation:

· 100% = farmer outperformed expectations
· 80–100% = average
· <80% = underperforming (requires investigation)

---

2.3 Payback Period (Farm Level)

How long it takes for the farmer to repay the capital raised (from harvest).

Formula:

\text{Payback Period} = \frac{\text{Capital Raised (K)}}{\text{Annual Net Profit}}

Net profit = farmer's share of harvest revenue minus input costs.

Why it matters:

· Shorter payback = lower risk for investors.
· Used to set farmer credit limits for future seasons.

---

2.4 Season‑over‑Season Growth (Farmer Skill Score)

Tracks improvement across multiple seasons.

Formula:

\text{Growth Score} = \frac{\text{Yield}_{t} - \text{Yield}_{t-1}}{\text{Yield}_{t-1}} \times 100

Weighted average across last 3 seasons → farmer skill rating (0–100).

What to tell Replit:

Compute a Farmer Skill Score based on yield growth, cost efficiency, and harvest quality. Display on farm listing pages to help investors choose.

---

3. Platform‑Level Calculations

3.1 Total Value Locked (TVL)

Total capital invested across all active farms.

Formula:

\text{TVL} = \sum \text{Capital Raised per Farm}

What to tell Replit:

Calculate and display TVL on the platform dashboard. Track TVL growth over time.

---

3.2 Platform Revenue Run Rate

Projected annual platform revenue based on current fee structures.

Formula:

\text{Revenue Run Rate} = \text{Total Fees (last 30 days)} \times 12

Breaks down by fee type:

· Primary purchase fees
· Secondary trading fees
· Withdrawal fees
· Portfolio management fees (platform share)

---

3.3 Investor Retention Rate

Percentage of investors who reinvest after their first harvest.

Formula:

\text{Retention} = \frac{\text{Investors with multiple seasons}}{\text{Total Investors}} \times 100

What to tell Replit:

Track retention by cohort (e.g., investors who joined in Q1 2026). This helps measure product‑market fit.

---

3.4 Capital Efficiency Ratio

How much investor capital is deployed vs sitting idle in wallets.

Formula:

\text{Capital Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Total Invested in Farms}}{\text{Total Cash Deposits}} \times 100

Target: >80% deployed means platform is healthy.

What to tell Replit:

Monitor capital efficiency daily. If too low, encourage investors to invest or launch more farms.

---

3.5 Average Farm Subscription Time

How long it takes for a farm to become fully subscribed.

Formula:

\text{Time to Full} = \text{Date Fully Subscribed} - \text{Launch Date}

Used for:

· Pricing strategy (if farms fill in <24 hours, increase supply or raise share price)
· Marketing effectiveness

---

4. Risk & Stress Testing

4.1 Value at Risk (VaR) for Investors

Estimates the maximum loss an investor could face with 95% confidence.

Formula:

\text{VaR}_{95\%} = \mu_p - 1.65 \times \sigma_p

· \mu_p = expected portfolio return
· \sigma_p = standard deviation of portfolio returns
· Multiply by portfolio value to get dollar amount

What to tell Replit:

Provide VaR on portfolio dashboard. Disclaim that past performance doesn't guarantee future results.

---

4.2 Stress Test Scenarios

Simulate how a portfolio would perform under extreme conditions.

Scenario Assumptions
Drought Revenue drops 50% for all farms in affected region
Price crash Crop prices drop 30% at harvest
Pest outbreak Yield drops 40% for specific crop type

What to tell Replit:

Build a stress test tool that adjusts forecast revenue and shows impact on investor returns.

---

4.3 Liquidity Risk (Secondary Market)

Measures how easily investors can exit without moving the price.

Formula:

\text{Liquidity Ratio} = \frac{\text{Total Open Orders (Bid + Ask)}}{\text{Total Shares Outstanding}}

Interpretation:

· 10% = highly liquid
· 2–10% = moderate
· <2% = illiquid (investors may struggle to exit)

What to tell Replit:

Display liquidity ratio for each farm. If ratio drops below 2%, send alert to platform ops.

---

5. Stellar & Blockchain Calculations

5.1 Transaction Fee Estimation

Stellar transaction fees are tiny (~0.00001 XLM per operation), but you need to calculate and deduct them.

Formula:

\text{Fee (XLM)} = \text{Base Fee} \times \text{Number of Operations}

What to tell Replit:

Estimate Stellar fees before each transaction. Deduct from investor's wallet or platform operational wallet.

5.2 Claimable Balance Expiry Monitoring

Claimable balances have a time lock (e.g., 30 days). You need to track them and notify investors before expiry.

What to tell Replit:

Run a daily job that checks all claimable balances. For those expiring in 7 days, send an email/SMS reminder to the investor to claim their harvest payout.

5.3 Trustline Monitoring

Investors must establish a trustline to receive farm tokens. Track who has/hasn't.

What to tell Replit:

When an investor buys shares, check if they have a trustline to the farm's asset issuer. If not, prompt them to establish one before completing the purchase.
