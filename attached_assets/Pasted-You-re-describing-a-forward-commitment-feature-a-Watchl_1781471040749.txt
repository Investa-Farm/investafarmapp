You're describing a forward commitment feature – a "Watchlist with locked-in pricing" where investors commit funds now to buy farm shares in the future at a predetermined price (usually a discount to the expected future market price). This benefits investors by capturing upside, and benefits the platform/farmer by securing early capital.

Let me give you the mathematical model and feature spec for this Commitment Watchlist.

---

1. Core Idea

· An investor commits, say, $500 today into a commitment contract for a specific farm season.
· The funds are held in escrow (or a wallet) and will be automatically used to buy shares at a fixed future date (e.g., 30 days before harvest) at a guaranteed price P_{\text{commit}}.
· The guaranteed price is set at a discount to the expected future spot price at that time, so investors gain if the price rises.
· If the investor changes their mind, they may forfeit a small penalty (e.g., 5% of committed funds) – or the commitment is non‑refundable after a grace period.

---

2. Mathematical Model

Let:

· t_0 = current time (when commitment is made)
· t_c = time of automatic purchase (e.g., 30 days before harvest)
· T = harvest time
· P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) = expected spot price at t_c (computed by the secondary market pricing model)
· P_{\text{commit}} = guaranteed purchase price for committed investors
· \delta = discount factor (e.g., 0.90 means 10% discount)

Definition:

P_{\text{commit}} = P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) \times (1 - \delta)

Where 0 < \delta < 1. The platform chooses \delta (say 5–15%) to incentivize early commitment.

How P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) is estimated today:
Use the secondary market pricing model with time to harvest = T - t_c and risk score forecast at t_c (could use current AI risk score unless expected to change significantly). For simplicity, assume risk score constant.

From earlier secondary seller price formula (ignoring liquidity & demand for this forecast):

P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) = \frac{\alpha \hat{R}(t_c)}{N (1+r_f)^{(T-t_c)/365}} \times (1 - \lambda(t_c))

Where \hat{R}(t_c) is forecast revenue at t_c (may be updated by AI). But at t_0, we use current forecast \hat{R}(t_0) and project forward using expected growth/decline. Often simpler: assume \hat{R} constant.

---

3. Investor’s Incentive & Break‑Even

If an investor commits C dollars today, they will receive at t_c:

\text{Number of shares} = \frac{C}{P_{\text{commit}}}

At harvest, their proceeds (if they hold) = shares × dividend D. But they could also sell the shares immediately at t_c on secondary market for P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) (assuming no liquidity discount). Their profit from flipping:

\text{Profit} = \text{shares} \times (P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) - P_{\text{commit}})

But since P_{\text{commit}} = P_{\text{spot}}(t_c)(1-\delta), the profit = \frac{C}{P_{\text{commit}}} \times \delta P_{\text{spot}}(t_c) = C \times \frac{\delta}{1-\delta}.

Example: \delta = 0.10 (10% discount) → profit = C \times 0.10/0.90 = 11.11\% immediate return (on paper) at t_c.

Thus investors are enticed to commit early.

4.3 Automated Execution

At t_c, the system:

· Uses committed funds to buy shares at P_{\text{commit}} (no further action by investor).
· Shares appear in investor’s portfolio.
· Any remaining uncommitted funds (if price changed? No – exact share count = amount / P_{\text{commit}}).

If the investor already sold the commitment (some platforms allow trading commitments), the new buyer assumes the obligation.

---

5. Risk Management for the Platform

· The platform must hedge the discount. If too many investors commit and spot price at t_c is actually lower than P_{\text{commit}} (e.g., due to bad weather forecast), the platform takes a loss.
· Solution: Set \delta conservatively, or use a dynamic discount linked to the volatility of the farm’s risk score. Higher risk = lower discount.
· Alternatively, the platform can sell call options to investors: investor pays a premium now for the right to buy at P_{\text{commit}} later. Then the platform caps downside.

But for simplicity, start with small \delta (5%) and limit total commitment per farm.

---

6. Summary Formula for Replit Implementation

Given current secondary fair price P_{\text{fair}}(t_c) (forecasted for the purchase date), the commitment price is:

\boxed{P_{\text{commit}} = P_{\text{fair}}(t_c) \times (1 - \delta)}

Where P_{\text{fair}}(t_c) is computed using the secondary market model with remaining days = T - t_c.
The number of shares an investor gets at purchase = \frac{\text{committed\_amount}}{P_{\text{commit}}}.
