3. ROI Calculation – Full Season Exit (Hold to Harvest)

3.1 Cost Basis (Entry)

\text{Cost} = q \times P_0 + (q \times P_0 \times 0.015)


Or simply:

\text{Cost} = q \times P_0 \times 1.015

3.2 Harvest Payout

At harvest, the investor receives:

\text{Payout} = q \times \frac{\alpha \times R_{\text{actual}}}{N}


Where:

· R_{\text{actual}} is the real harvest revenue (not the forecast).
· This depends on actual yield (affected by rainfall, NDVI) and actual commodity price.

3.3 Realized ROI (Full Season)

\text{ROI}_{\text{full}} = \frac{\text{Payout}}{\text{Cost}} - 1

3.4 Annualized ROI (Full Season)

\text{Annualized ROI}_{\text{full}} = (1 + \text{ROI}_{\text{full}})^{\frac{365}{T}} - 1


Where T = days from planting to harvest (e.g., 240 days).

Example:

· P_0 = \$10, q = 100 shares → Cost = 1000 \times 1.015 = \$1,015
· \alpha = 0.20, N = 1,500, R_{\text{actual}} = \$120,000
· Dividend per share = (0.20 \times 120,000) / 1,500 = \$16
· Payout = 100 \times \$16 = \$1,600
· ROI = 1600/1015 - 1 = 57.6\%
· Annualized (if T=240 days) = (1.576)^{365/240} - 1 = (1.576)^{1.52} - 1 = 97.6\% annually

4. ROI Calculation – Mid‑Season Exit (30–60 Days)

4.1 Cost Basis (Entry) – Same as above

\text{Cost} = q \times P_0 \times 1.015

4.2 Sale Proceeds (Secondary Market)

At mid‑season (day t, where t = 30–60 days), the investor sells all q shares on the secondary market.

\text{Sale Proceeds} = q \times P_{\text{sell}}(t) \times (1 - 0.005)


Where:

· P_{\text{sell}}(t) is the seller’s price from our secondary market formula:
  P_{\text{sell}}(t) = \frac{\alpha \hat{R}(t)}{N (1 + r_f)^{(T-t)/365}} \times (1 - \lambda(t)) \times (1 + \beta \cdot \text{imbalance}) \times (1 - \delta)
· \hat{R}(t) = AI‑updated revenue forecast at time t (incorporating rainfall, NDVI changes).
· \lambda(t) = updated risk discount based on new risk score.
· The 0.005 is the secondary trading fee (0.5%).

4.3 Realized ROI (Mid‑Season)

\text{ROI}_{\text{mid}} = \frac{\text{Sale Proceeds}}{\text{Cost}} - 1

4.4 Annualized ROI (Mid‑Season)

\text{Annualized ROI}_{\text{mid}} = (1 + \text{ROI}_{\text{mid}})^{\frac{365}{t}} - 1


Where t = days held (e.g., 45).

Example (Mid‑Season Exit at Day 45):

· Same cost basis: $1,015.
· At day 45, NDVI dropped due to mild drought. AI updates:
  · \hat{R}(45) = \$85,000 (was $120,000)
  · Risk score drops from 7 → 5
  · \lambda(45) = 0.25
  · T-t = 195 days left
  · d = (1-0.25)/(1.10)^{195/365} = 0.75 / 1.051 = 0.713
  · Fair value before imbalance = (0.20 \times 85,000)/(1,500) \times 0.713 = 11.33 \times 0.713 = \$8.08
  · Assume imbalance factor = 1.0 (neutral), liquidity discount = 0.02 → P_{\text{sell}} = 8.08 \times 0.98 = \$7.92
· Sale proceeds = 100 \times 7.92 \times (1 - 0.005) = 792 \times 0.995 = \$788.04
· ROI = 788.04 / 1015 - 1 = -22.4\%
· Annualized = (0.776)^{365/45} - 1 = (0.776)^{8.11} - 1 = -90.7\% (huge negative annualized, reflecting that holding would have been disastrous)

Counter‑Example (Positive Mid‑Season Exit):

· If rain is abundant and NDVI soars, \hat{R}(45) = \$150,000, risk score improves to 9.
· P_{\text{sell}} = \text{higher} → sale proceeds might be $1,400 → ROI = 38% in 45 days → annualized = (1.38)^{8.11} - 1 = 1,380\% (massive, because you captured a great early signal).

7. The No‑Arbitrage Relationship Between the Two

In an efficient market, the expected annualised ROI of holding to harvest should equal the expected annualised ROI of selling mid‑season (after accounting for risk and fees). If they diverge, arbitrage exists.

Arbitrage Condition:

(1 + \text{Expected ROI}_{\text{full}})^{\frac{365}{T}} \approx (1 + \text{Expected ROI}_{\text{mid}})^{\frac{365}{t}} \times \text{Risk Premium}

What the AI does:

· The AI’s fair value P_{\text{fair}}(t) is specifically designed to make this equation hold given current information.
· If P_{\text{sell}}(t) is below fair value, the mid‑season ROI becomes higher than the full‑season ROI (annualised). That signals a BUY opportunity (or HOLD).
· If P_{\text{sell}}(t) is above fair value, the mid‑season ROI is lower – that signals a SELL opportunity.

This is exactly what your No‑Arbitrage AI Check (from earlier) monitors.
