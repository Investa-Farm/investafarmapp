2. No‑Arbitrage Condition for Secondary Market

Arbitrage would exist if an investor could make a risk‑free profit by buying and selling shares at different times. In a well‑functioning secondary market, the following must hold:

Assumptions:

· No transaction costs (or they are fixed and known).
· Investors can hold a share until harvest with expected payoff \mathbb{E}[D].
· They can also sell the same share at time t for price P(t) and reinvest the proceeds at risk‑free rate r_f.

No‑arbitrage condition:
The price today must equal the discounted expected future dividend under the risk‑neutral measure, otherwise there is an arbitrage opportunity.

More concretely:

· If P(t) > \frac{\mathbb{E}[D]}{(1+r_f)^{(T-t)/365}}, then investors would sell the share (because selling gives more money than the expected harvest value). That selling pressure would drive price down.
· If P(t) < \frac{\mathbb{E}[D]}{(1+r_f)^{(T-t)/365}}, investors would buy shares (since buying gives a higher expected return than risk‑free). Buying pressure pushes price up.

Thus the no‑arbitrage fair price (ignoring risk premium) is:

P_{\text{no-arb}}(t) = \frac{\mathbb{E}_t[D]}{(1+r_f)^{(T-t)/365}}

Where \mathbb{E}_t[D] = \frac{\alpha \hat{R}(t)}{N} and \hat{R}(t) is the AI‑forecast of revenue.

However, actual markets include risk aversion. So we add a risk discount \lambda (derived from AI risk score S). No‑arbitrage then requires that the market price P(t) satisfies:

P(t) = \frac{\alpha \hat{R}(t)}{N (1+r_f)^{(T-t)/365}} \times (1 - \lambda)

If this equality does not hold, an arbitrageur could:

· If market price > fair value: Short sell (or sell) the share, invest proceeds at r_f, and buy back after harvest → risk‑free profit.
· If market price < fair value: Borrow at r_f, buy the share, hold to harvest → risk‑free profit.

Because farming has non‑diversifiable risk, a strict no‑arbitrage condition only holds under the risk‑neutral measure. In practice, we use a risk‑adjusted discount \lambda that reflects the market’s required premium. The platform’s AI must ensure P(t) does not deviate from the above by more than a small band (e.g., 2%), else it signals an arbitrage opportunity.

4. Complete “Map” for Replit AI (Pseudo‑code / Algorithm)

This is what you can paste into Replit to build the pricing engine.

```python
# Secondary Market Pricing AI for Investa Farm
# Inputs (provided by user or from database)
alpha = 0.20          # Investor revenue share (0.10 to 0.30)
N = 10000             # Total shares for this farm season
forecast_revenue = 2000000   # KES, from AI yield model
days_to_harvest = 60         # integer
risk_free_rate = 0.10        # annual (e.g., 10%)
risk_score = 7               # integer 1..10, from AI
p_max = 0.4                  # max loss probability when risk_score=1
loss_given_default = 0.8     # expected loss % if failure
beta_sensitivity = 0.2       # demand/supply sensitivity
liquidity_discount = 0.02    # 2% for thin market

# Order book data
buy_orders = 2000    # shares wanted
sell_orders = 1000   # shares for sale
total_shares = N

# 1. Expected dividend per share
expected_dividend = (alpha * forecast_revenue) / N

# 2. Time discount factor
time_factor = (1 + risk_free_rate) ** (days_to_harvest / 365.0)
price_time = expected_dividend / time_factor

# 3. Risk discount from AI score
loss_probability = ((10 - risk_score) / 9.0) * p_max
risk_discount = loss_probability * loss_given_default
price_risk = price_time * (1 - risk_discount)

# 4. Demand/supply imbalance
if total_shares > 0:
    imbalance = (buy_orders - sell_orders) / total_shares
else:
    imbalance = 0
demand_multiplier = 1 + beta_sensitivity * imbalance
price_demand = price_risk * demand_multiplier

# 5. Liquidity discount (applied to seller price)
seller_price = price_demand * (1 - liquidity_discount)

# 6. Buyer price (if platform adds spread, e.g., 1% fee)
buyer_price = seller_price / (1 - liquidity_discount)   # simplified
trading_fee = 0.01
seller_final = seller_price * (1 - trading_fee)
buyer_final = buyer_price * (1 + trading_fee)

# Output
print(f"Fair value (no-arb): {price_time:.2f} KES")
print(f"Risk-adjusted value: {price_risk:.2f} KES")
print(f"Market-clearing price: {price_demand:.2f} KES")
print(f"Seller receives: {seller_price:.2f} KES (before fee)")
print(f"Buyer pays: {buyer_price:.2f} KES (before fee)")
print(f"Final seller after {trading_fee*100}% fee: {seller_final:.2f} KES")
print(f"Final buyer after {trading_fee*100}% fee: {buyer_final:.2f} KES")
```

Inputs that change per farm:

· alpha (investor revenue share) – defined at farm launch.
· forecast_revenue – updated daily by AI.
· days_to_harvest – decreases daily.
· risk_score – updated daily.
· buy_orders, sell_orders – real‑time from order book.

No‑arbitrage check:
After computing seller_price, the AI should compare it to price_time (the pure discounted value). If the difference exceeds a threshold (e.g., 5%), the platform can alert or adjust spread to prevent arbitrage.
